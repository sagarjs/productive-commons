# The Productive Commons — Key Tenets

A labor-equity economic framework: ownership follows contribution, not capital. The through-line of every tenet below is that **equity is the residual claimant** — expressed consistently in valuation (stock), dividends (flow), buyback (exit), and failure (liability), all on a net-of-reserves basis, all risk/reward symmetric.

---

## Definitions

These terms are referenced across the tenets below.

- **Enterprise value (EV)** — the lower of 3× revenue or 5× profit, floored at zero.
- **Net liabilities** — total liabilities minus qualifying reserves.
- **Qualifying reserves** — cash and near-cash only, liquid and unencumbered. Receivables, inventory, and pledged assets do not count.
- **Near-term coverage floor** — reserves must cover near-term liabilities in full first; only the surplus nets against longer-dated debt.
- **Equity value** — `max(0, EV − net liabilities)`. The residual claim used for all buyback and dividend math.

---

## Tenets

1. **State owns, labor executes.** All industries are nationalized; operations are delegated to labor-equity corporations via competitive tender.

2. **Equity through labor only.** 50% of all compensation is equity, 50% cash. Equity can never be bought, sold, traded, gifted, or inherited between individuals.

3. **Buyback as the only exit.** Corporations buy back equity from the 10% buyback pool, triggered solely by the holder. No equity markets exist. The buyback price is the holder's equity fraction × **equity value** (Tenet 4) — net-liability-adjusted, never gross valuation. Buybacks **freeze automatically** when audited distress signals trip (net liabilities ≥ EV, or any default event) and remain frozen through resolution.

4. **Formula-bound valuation.** EV = the lower of 3× revenue or 5× profit, floored at zero. **Equity value = `max(0, EV − net liabilities)`.** All buyback and dividend math uses equity value, never gross EV — equity is the residual claim, and gross EV overstates it by the firm's debt (the source of phantom value at the failure cliff). No speculative pricing.

5. **Reserves and resiliency.** Equity is valued and dividends are gated on **net** liabilities, not gross. Firms hold qualifying reserves against their obligations. Profit may be distributed only when reserves cover obligations (net liabilities ≤ 0); otherwise profit is swept in order — (1) near-term liability coverage, (2) reserves to obligation level, (3) loan principal. This privileges resiliency over capital efficiency by design.

6. **Fixed financial waterfall.** Revenue (income minus non-salary expenses) is distributed: 10% buyback pool, 10% individual government pension, 30% taxes, 50% corporate income. Profit (corporate income minus salaries) pays ≥50% as dividend pro rata by equity — **but the dividend is suspended while net liabilities exceed zero** (Tenet 5) and resumes once reserves cover obligations. Creditors rank ahead of equity distributions (absolute priority).

7. **20:1 compensation ratio.** Recommended maximum-to-minimum total compensation ratio, voted annually — one vote per employee, one vote per non-employee holder with ≥1% equity.

8. **Full public disclosure.** All compensation, transactions, audits, liabilities, and reserves are on a public ledger. Citizen auditors earn bounties for verified findings — **including reserve misclassification and liability understatement.**

9. **Deposits stored, never lent.** Banking is custodial only. Lending is a separate, risk-bearing activity with no state guarantee.

10. **Symmetric risk for facilitators.** Any intermediary sharing in lending profit bears the same percentage of loss.

11. **50% principal protection on default.** Half of lender principal is protected (passed as liability to equity holders, per Tenet 12); half is written off as shared loss per pre-agreed terms.

12. **No bailouts, ever.** On failure: equity → zero; liabilities split 50% NPA / 50% distributed to equity holders as personal liability, **pro rata by equity.** The distribution is **time-weighted over a trailing 12-month look-back**: holders who exited via buyback inside the window remain liable in proportion to their time-weighted holdings — exiting before failure does not shed the liability share. The state re-absorbs and re-tenders.

13. **Liability follows the individual.** Post-failure personal liability attaches to all future income until discharged.

14. **Competitive multi-provider tendering.** Government services require multiple providers, selected by consumer feedback, audit results, and price.
