# The Productive Commons — Key Tenets

1. **State owns, labor executes.** All industries are nationalized; operations delegated to labor-equity corporations via competitive tender.

2. **Equity through labor only.** 50% of all compensation is equity, 50% cash. Equity can never be bought, sold, traded, gifted, or inherited between individuals.

3. **Buyback as the only exit.** Corporations buy back equity from the 10% buyback pool, triggered solely by the holder. No equity markets exist.

4. **Formula-bound valuation.** Equity valued at the lower of 3× revenue or 5× profit (positive values only). No speculative pricing.

5. **Fixed financial waterfall.** Revenue (income minus non-salary expenses) is distributed: 10% buyback pool, 10% individual govt pension, 30% taxes, 50% corporate income. Profit (corporate income minus salaries) pays ≥50% as dividend pro rata by equity.

6. **20:1 compensation ratio.** Recommended max-to-min total compensation ratio, voted annually — one vote per employee, one vote per non-employee holder with ≥1% equity.

7. **Full public disclosure.** All compensation, transactions, and audits on a public ledger. Citizen auditors earn bounties for verified findings.

8. **Deposits stored, never lent.** Banking is custodial only. Lending is a separate, risk-bearing activity with no state guarantee.

9. **Symmetric risk for facilitators.** Any intermediary sharing in lending profit bears the same percentage of loss.

10. **50% principal protection on default.** Half of lender principal is protected (passed as liability to equity holders); half is written off as shared loss per pre-agreed terms.

11. **No bailouts, ever.** On failure: equity → zero, liabilities split 50% NPA / 50% distributed to equity holders as personal liability. State re-absorbs and re-tenders.

12. **Liability follows the individual.** Post-failure personal liability attaches to all future income until discharged.

13. **Competitive multi-provider tendering.** Government services require multiple providers, selected by consumer feedback, audit results, and price.
